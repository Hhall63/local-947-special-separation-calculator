Before you begin

The Special Separation Allowance requires every condition below. You can still use the calculator to see which conditions pass and which do not.

Have your retirement year, birth month and year, current GFD and other service, sick hours, and salary or rank information ready.

Your entries stay in this browser. If you use the salary lookup, this page retrieves public Fire salary records from the City and searches names locally. Reloading this page clears your entries and the retrieved records.

Review definitions and retirement rules in the official Local Governmental Employees’ Retirement System (LGERS) Member Handbook (opens in a new tab).

  • Retire under the Local Governmental Employees’ Retirement System (LGERS) on a regular, unreduced service retirement—not an early reduced or disability retirement.
  • Be under age 62 on your January 31 retirement date.
  • Complete at least five continuous years as a sworn Greensboro firefighter immediately before retirement.
  • Have at least 50% of total creditable service as sworn Greensboro firefighter service.

        Calculation assumptions
        • Retirement occurs January 31.
        • Sick leave accrues at no more than eight hours per month.
        • Current sick hours are projected using your uncapped historical net rate. The rate is based on current sick hours and completed GFD and other LGERS service, then applied through retirement.
        • November 1 is used as the estimated annual raise date because the dates City Hall releases raises vary.
        • Salary values use the FY 2025-2026 structure effective October 15, 2025 until this calculator is manually updated.
        • Nonexempt progression stops at Step 5 for F01 or Step 8 for F02-F05.
        • Exempt merit defaults to 4%, is editable and discretionary, and stops at Green Zone Maximum.
        • Every modeled promotion into an exempt rank starts at Green Zone Minimum.
        • The allowance multiplier is 0.0085.
        • Gross biweekly allowance uses 26 payments per year.
